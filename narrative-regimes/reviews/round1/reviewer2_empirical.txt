Reviewer #2 (Empirical Finance / Financial Econometrics)
Manuscript: "Narrative Regimes: LLM-Augmented Strategic Asset Allocation for Long-Horizon Investors"
Venue: Decision Support Systems

==================================================================
SUMMARY
==================================================================
The manuscript proposes an online Bayesian regime filter that fuses an LLM-
distilled narrative likelihood with a return-based Gaussian likelihood, plugged
into a regime-policy-mix CRRA allocator, evaluated on a three-regime Gaussian
Monte-Carlo testbed. The mechanical contribution (filter + allocator) is clean
and reproducible, and the SNR-threshold framing is genuinely useful. However,
the empirical case for the headline gains is not airtight: several numbers in
the abstract and running text do not match the replication tables I was asked
to audit, the transaction-cost evidence is not backed by the data in
results/tables/table_tc.csv, and confidence intervals are essentially absent.

==================================================================
MAJOR COMMENTS
==================================================================

M1. Headline Sharpe figures in the abstract and Section 5.1 text do not
    match Table 1 (nor the underlying CSV).
    The abstract and Section 5.1 claim "Sharpe 0.30 vs 0.23 (60/40)" and
    "Sharpe 0.30 vs. 0.23 (60/40), 0.22 (1/N), 0.28 (risk parity), 0.24
    (rolling MV)." But the paper's own Table 1 (line 349) reports
    Narrative Regimes Sharpe = 0.257, and results/tables/table_main.csv
    gives sharpe_mean = 0.2567 (SD = 0.1997). RiskParity's sharpe_mean in
    the CSV is 0.2772 - i.e. risk parity STRICTLY DOMINATES Narrative
    Regimes on the paper's own primary risk-adjusted metric. The paper
    briefly acknowledges the risk-parity comparison in CRRA-CE but the
    abstract's Sharpe claim is simply incorrect. This is a serious
    problem: an abstract that overstates the headline risk-adjusted
    metric by ~17% relative and implies dominance over all baselines
    when the data show sub-dominance vs. risk parity must be corrected
    before any further review.

M2. The transaction-cost sweep is not honestly presented; the table does
    not vary with tc_bps.
    results/tables/table_tc.csv contains tc_bps in {0, 5, 10, 20, 50},
    but the annual_return, annual_vol, sharpe, sortino, avg_turnover,
    and crra_ce_annual columns are byte-identical across every tc_bps
    level - only cagr, max_drawdown, and calmar vary. Mechanically this
    suggests that TC is being applied only to the cumulative equity
    curve used for drawdown/CAGR, but NOT deducted from per-period
    returns before computing Sharpe/Sortino/CRRA-CE - a specification
    bug that invalidates the paper's core robustness claim. Moreover,
    Section 5.4's narrative (Narrative Regimes CRRA-CE "declines from
    6.7% at tau=0 bps to 6.2%, 4.3%, and 2.0% at tau=10, 50, 100 bps
    respectively") cannot be reproduced from the released table: at
    seed-900-like levels (NarrativeRegimes, crra_ce_annual = 0.0665),
    the value is constant across all tc_bps in the file. The discussion
    appears to be based on numbers the reader cannot verify. Either
    the TC evaluation pipeline must be fixed and the table regenerated,
    or the TC discussion must be deleted.

M3. No standard errors / confidence intervals, yet many effect sizes are
    smaller than one Monte-Carlo SD.
    With n_paths = 30 and sharpe_std = 0.1997 (NarrativeRegimes), the
    Monte-Carlo SE on the mean Sharpe is ~0.036, giving an approximate
    95% CI of [0.186, 0.328]. The SE on the Sharpe difference vs.
    RiskParity (assuming independent paths - the paper uses the same
    30 seeds, so differences should be computed path-wise) is even
    tighter, but the point estimate itself favors RiskParity. Similarly
    the 11 pp accuracy gain (0.608 vs. 0.498 with SDs 0.160 and 0.222)
    has a two-sample SE of roughly (0.160^2/30 + 0.222^2/30)^0.5 ~=
    0.050; the gap is meaningful (z ~ 2.2) but not overwhelming, and
    paired inference on the same paths would be more informative. I
    need to see paired t-tests or bootstrap CIs on every reported
    difference; the current presentation invites over-interpretation.

M4. Fairness of baselines is uneven.
    (i) Rolling MV: window length, shrinkage intensity, and whether it
    is also subject to the 0.7 inertia smoother and the [0, w_bar] cap
    applied to Narrative Regimes are not disclosed. (ii) The Narrative
    Regimes allocator uses per-regime shrinkage toward a grand mean
    (parameter alpha in Section 3.3) but the value of alpha is never
    given; rolling MV presumably has none. (iii) Risk parity uses
    inverse-volatility; a minimum-variance and an HRP (Lopez de Prado)
    baseline would be standard in any 2025 allocation paper. (iv) The
    ablation "RegimeOnly" uses the same filter with only the return
    likelihood - good - but its moments and transition-matrix estimator
    are identical to the narrative version, which means the ablation
    measures ONLY the marginal information value of the narrative
    likelihood at a given filter hyperparameterisation, not of regime-
    switching vs. unconditional MV. The paper blurs this distinction.

M5. External validity of the DGP is weakly supported.
    The Gaussian-per-regime DGP with diagonal(P) = (0.95, 0.82, 0.60)
    is calibrated by hand in Appendix A with seven-asset means and
    vols chosen a priori. Three substantive gaps: (a) no heavy tails
    (Cont 2001), (b) no skew or asymmetric-vol crash dynamics,
    (c) per-regime correlation matrices are referenced as being in
    the replication bundle but not stated in Appendix A (readers
    cannot audit the critical cross-asset correlation structure, which
    drives most of the allocator's tilts). Realism would be greatly
    improved by either (i) matching the first four moments of a
    multi-decade benchmark (e.g. Kenneth French factors + FRED),
    (ii) validating that the simulated expansion/contraction/stress
    shares and durations are consistent with NBER, or (iii) running
    one Student-t stress test to show the ranking is invariant.
    Without any of these, the discussion-section appeal to external
    validity is hand-waving.

M6. The look-ahead-freedom claim is simulation-definitional, not
    methodological.
    The paper claims look-ahead bias is "eliminated by construction".
    True, the SIMULATION has no look-ahead. But the claim the reader
    cares about is whether a real LLM pipeline, parameterised at
    beta, would be look-ahead-free, and the answer is obviously no.
    The simulation replaces the LLM with a Gaussian signal whose
    precision is imposed. This is a valid sensitivity study; it is
    not, and cannot be, evidence that an LLM deployment would avoid
    look-ahead. The abstract's "eliminating look-ahead bias by
    construction" and the contribution-list framing conflate these
    two claims and should be softened.

==================================================================
MINOR COMMENTS
==================================================================

m1. Effect sizes. Only Sharpe, max DD, and CRRA-CE are reported in
    prose. Please add a utility-equivalent return (fee equivalent
    an investor would pay to switch from 60/40 to NR) and a paired
    Diebold-Mariano-style test on monthly utility contributions.

m2. Seed disclosure. Main experiment uses seeds 100-129 (30 seeds),
    SNR sweep 500-519 (20 seeds), TC sweep 900-919, gamma sweep
    1300-1319. These ranges are disjoint and reasonable, but the
    paper should state explicitly whether seeds were chosen before
    any results were inspected; otherwise the reader cannot rule out
    specification search at the seed level. A one-line preregistration
    statement would suffice.

m3. Representative-seed prose (Section 5.4, seed 900; Section 5.5,
    seed 42). Both are single-seed anecdotes used to illustrate
    results. Either remove them or report the full 20-seed
    distribution in an appendix.

m4. Table 3 (SNR). The CRRA-CE for NarrativeRegimes at beta=1.00 is
    reported as 0.0181; the mean across the 20 seeds in
    table_snr.csv for snr=1.0, strategy=NarrativeRegimes should be
    re-checked - it appears to round to ~0.018 but several individual
    seeds are negative (e.g. -0.024) and the SD is large; please
    add the cross-seed SD column.

m5. Turnover accounting. Table 1 shows NarrativeRegimes avg_turnover
    = 0.305 (SD = 0.220), an order of magnitude above the
    inertia-smoothed 60/40 or risk parity (~0.03). A reader is
    entitled to know whether the tau=10 bp cost deducted is computed
    on the gross turnover or on a semi-rebalance definition; please
    add the one-line definition used in the equity-curve construction.

RECOMMENDATION
==================================================================
MAJOR REVISION

==================================================================
JUSTIFICATION
==================================================================
The framework is conceptually sound and the SNR-threshold framing is a
genuine contribution that fits Decision Support Systems well. But the
empirical story as written contains (a) a numerically incorrect headline
Sharpe claim in the abstract (M1), (b) a transaction-cost sweep whose
released CSV does not support the paper's discussion (M2), (c) no
uncertainty quantification despite small n_paths (M3), and (d) an
over-reach on the look-ahead-freedom claim (M6). None of these is
fatal - M1 is a text fix, M2 is a pipeline fix, M3 is a trivial
bootstrap - but together they preclude acceptance in current form.
I would be glad to see a revised version once the tables and the text
agree, the TC experiment is re-run with costs deducted at the per-period
level, and every reported difference is accompanied by a paired standard
error or bootstrap CI. The DGP-realism concerns (M5) and baseline-fairness
concerns (M4) are genuine but can be addressed in a revision by adding
a Student-t robustness cell and disclosing all baseline hyperparameters.
