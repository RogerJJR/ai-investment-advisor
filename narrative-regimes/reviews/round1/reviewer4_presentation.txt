================================================================================
Reviewer #4 Report - Senior Editor / Writing & Presentation Specialist
Manuscript: "Narrative Regimes: LLM-Augmented Strategic Asset Allocation for
            Long-Horizon Investors"
Target venue: Decision Support Systems (Elsevier)
Date of review: 2026-04-20
================================================================================

SUMMARY
-------
The manuscript proposes "Narrative Regimes," a framework that couples an online
Bayesian regime filter with an LLM-distilled narrative likelihood and a CRRA
regime-policy-mix allocator, evaluated on a simulation-based testbed that
eliminates look-ahead bias by construction. The paper is readable, well
structured, and deliberately written for the DSS audience. The abstract, the
contribution list, and the discussion all connect the statistical machinery to
an explicit adoption decision (SNR threshold). LaTeX appears to compile cleanly
under elsarticle with standard packages. From a presentation standpoint the
paper is closer to publishable than not; the remaining issues are largely about
sharpening the DSS framing, integrating the AI-usage declaration properly, and
tightening a handful of writing and figure-caption details.

MAJOR COMMENTS
--------------
1. Decision-support angle in the abstract is implicit rather than explicit.
   The abstract is informative and numerically rich, but the phrase "decision
   support" appears once and the reader has to infer what managerial decision
   the paper supports. DSS readers expect an opening sentence that names the
   decision (adopt or not adopt LLM signals in SAA; under what conditions) and
   the decision-maker (CIO, investment committee). Recommend rewriting the
   first two sentences to lead with the managerial question and then introduce
   the methodological response. The SNR threshold (beta* ~ 1) should be
   surfaced in the abstract as the core decision rule.

2. AI usage declaration should be integrated into the main text, not kept as
   a separate .md file. Elsevier's policy (and common practice at DSS)
   requires the disclosure to appear in the published manuscript, typically
   immediately before the references or as an unnumbered section after
   Conclusion. The current manuscript has a short subsection 5.3 that partly
   covers this, but the richer CRediT-aligned table in ai_usage_declaration.md
   is strictly more informative and should be promoted into the paper (or
   into an appendix titled "Declaration of generative AI in scientific
   writing"). At present a reader of the PDF does not see the table at all.
   This is a compliance issue, not just a stylistic one.

3. Contribution list overlaps with prior work on one axis and needs
   sharpening. Contribution (1) claims "the first" long-horizon SAA framework
   integrating LLM signals into a Bayesian regime filter. Given Chen et al.
   (2024) (Black-Litterman), Kim et al. (2025, HARLF), and Wang et al. (2025)
   already embed LLM signals in allocators, the novelty is really the
   *combination* of (i) Bayesian regime filter, (ii) CRRA regime-policy-mix,
   and (iii) simulation-based look-ahead-free evaluation. Recommend rewording
   to differentiate on these three axes explicitly rather than relying on "to
   the best of our knowledge." Also, contribution (4) restates numeric results
   that belong in the abstract; contributions should describe *what is new*,
   not *what the numbers are*.

4. Section order is appropriate, but the "Discussion" section is thin for a
   DSS submission and the "Implications for practice" subsection should be
   expanded and elevated. DSS expects a managerial-implications discussion
   that tells a CIO: (a) how to measure their LLM's SNR in practice, (b) how
   to update governance (audit trail, ablation testing), and (c) how the
   framework interacts with existing risk budgets and mandates. The current
   half-page subsection is a good outline but under-developed for the
   audience. Also, Conclusion is very short (one paragraph) and should be
   expanded to restate the decision rule, the scope of validity, and the
   next-step recommendation.

5. Tables and figures are not fully self-contained. Three concrete issues:
   (a) Table 1 caption does not define "Turnover" (monthly average? annualised?
       gross one-way?) or the units of CRRA CE (annualised decimal).
   (b) Table 3 has an awkward column order: "Accuracy gain," "Narrative
       CRRA-CE," "Regime CRRA-CE," and "Accuracy with narrative" are placed
       such that a reader cannot immediately verify the gain. Reorder to
       Regime CRRA-CE, Narrative CRRA-CE, delta, accuracy, delta.
   (c) Figures 1-3 captions should be readable without the text: Fig. 1 should
       name the baseline curve (RegimeOnly) explicitly in the caption; Fig. 2
       should explain what "true latent regime overlaid on the right axis"
       means (i.e., step function 1/2/3); Fig. 3 should state that bands are
       omitted because the between-strategy differences are visually clearer
       without them. Also verify the figure files (figures/fig_snr_curve.pdf
       etc.) are actually present in the submission bundle; the manuscript
       will fail to typeset otherwise.

6. Bibliography is adequate but missing several important recent references.
   For a 2026 submission to DSS on LLMs-in-finance, the reader will expect
   to see: (i) Yang et al. (FinGPT / FinBERT follow-ups), (ii) Fatouros et al.
   on LLM sentiment for trading, (iii) Chen/Pelger/Zhu on deep-learning asset
   pricing (widely cited baseline for ML in finance), (iv) the Kim, Muhn,
   Nikolaev 2024 on LLM-based financial statement analysis, and (v) a DSS-
   native citation (Feuerriegel et al., or prior DSS papers on NLP for
   financial decision support) to anchor the venue fit. Also: LopezLira2023
   is labelled as 2023 in-text but 2024 in the bib (cosmetic mismatch), and
   "Bollerslev2022NewsAttention" key refers to a 2015 JFQA paper - the key
   and/or the title should be corrected. Several entries use @article for
   books/proceedings (Campbell2002, MichaudMichaud2008, Chen2024LLMBL);
   change to @book / @inproceedings respectively to avoid elsarticle-harv
   formatting oddities.

MINOR COMMENTS
--------------
M1. Writing - passive voice and hedging. Several sentences in Sections 1-2
    use passive "is applied," "are computed," "has long embraced." These are
    acceptable but cluster in the introduction; consider activating at least
    one per paragraph ("We apply," "We compute") to keep energy.

M2. Orphaned notation and jargon.
    - "Welford recursions" (Sec. 3.2, Algorithm) is never defined; add a
      half-sentence gloss ("numerically stable online moment updates").
    - "Markov-modulated CRRA policy" appears twice without a brief definition.
    - Delta^{N-1} (the simplex) is used without introduction.
    - "shrunk toward their grand mean with weight alpha" - alpha is never
      given a value; specify or point to Appendix.
    - In Eq. (filter), the prior p(z_t=k | F_{t-1}) is denoted; later in
      Algorithm 1 it is written p_{t|t-1}. Unify notation.
    - beta^{\*} with escaped asterisk (line 391) will render literally -
      drop the backslash; use beta^{\star} or beta^* instead.

M3. Units and magnitudes. The abstract reports "2.4%", "1.1%", "0.7%";
    Table 1 reports 0.0236, 0.0110, 0.0066. These are consistent but a casual
    reader will not realise. Add a footnote to Table 1 stating that CRRA CE
    is a decimal annualised rate.

M4. Data availability placeholder. Line 549 still contains the template
    string "https://github.com/<your-username>/narrative-regimes" - must be
    resolved before camera-ready; flag to the author now. Same placeholder
    appears in ai_usage_declaration.md.

M5. Redundancy between Section 5.3 ("AI usage declaration") and the external
    .md file. Pick one canonical location; if the external file stays, the
    paper should cite it explicitly and summarise only the headline.

M6. Minor typos / style:
    - "regimes of parameter space" (line 111) - awkward; "regions of
      parameter space" is what is meant.
    - "The narrative filter strictly dominates" (line 388) - "strictly
      dominates" is a strong claim; the table shows it by mean but standard
      errors are not reported. Soften or add SEs.
    - "Two developments since 2022" (line 83) - date it more precisely
      ("Since the release of ChatGPT in late 2022...") for DSS readers who
      may not be ML-fluent.
    - "risk parity's case the difference is marginal" - be explicit that
      risk parity actually achieves a slightly higher CRRA CE than Narrative
      Regimes in Table 1 (0.0256 vs 0.0236); the abstract claim that
      Narrative Regimes is best on CRRA CE is contradicted by Table 1 and
      must be reconciled. This is borderline major - flagging here but the
      author should check carefully.

RECOMMENDATION
--------------
MINOR REVISION (bordering on MAJOR revision contingent on resolving the
CRRA CE inconsistency flagged in M6 and the AI-declaration integration
in Major Comment 2).

JUSTIFICATION
-------------
The manuscript is well-written, structurally sound, and makes a genuinely
decision-relevant contribution (an explicit SNR-based adoption rule) that
maps cleanly to the DSS audience. The methods are transparent, the ablation
is honest, and the replication posture is exemplary. The writing concerns
listed above are fixable in one revision cycle. The two material issues -
integrating the AI-usage declaration into the PDF (compliance) and
reconciling the CRRA CE claim between abstract and Table 1 (accuracy) -
are not large-scope changes, but they need to happen before publication.
If both are resolved cleanly, and the contribution list is sharpened to
differentiate from Chen (2024), Kim (2025), and Wang (2025), the paper
is a good fit for Decision Support Systems.
