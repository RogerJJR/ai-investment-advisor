Reviewer #1 Report (Round 5) - Methodology / Bayesian Filtering / Portfolio Optimization
Manuscript: "Narrative Regimes: LLM-Augmented Strategic Asset Allocation for Long-Horizon Investors"
Venue: Decision Support Systems

Summary
-------
The authors have preserved every element I signed off on in round 4 (Bayesian filter,
soft-assignment Welford, Jensen caveat, DP-gap bound, paired-MC SEs, CRRA-CE annualisation
disclosure, moment-mix ablation, Ang-Bekaert/Guidolin-Timmermann/Campbell-Viceira
citations) and added a new "Narrative Regimes (iterated)" variant driven by a 30-step
greedy hyper-parameter sweep (notebooks/iterate_roi.py; enhancements.py; allocator_v2.py).
The final composite enables Ledoit-Wolf-style covariance shrinkage (alpha=0.2),
regime-specific risk aversion, 8% annualised volatility targeting, mu-shrinkage=0.4,
weight inertia=0.85, and a 10% Treasury floor. Reported gains are modest (+27 bps CRRA-CE,
+1.8 pp Sharpe, -4 pp MDD, turnover halved). The abstract (lines 53-56) and new
subsection (lines 395-416) are appropriately restrained and explicitly credit vol
targeting as the dominant contributor.

New major comments
------------------
M1. Selection-bias disclosure for the greedy sweep is adequate but should be tightened.
    iterate_roi.py:60 uses seed_base=2000 (12 paths) for selection while the headline
    Table 1 uses seed_base=100 (30 paths) - these are disjoint, which is the right
    design and I commend the authors. Please state this explicitly in Section 4.5
    around line 400 ("the greedy sweep is evaluated on a disjoint 12-seed validation
    fold; Table 1 figures are computed on the 30-seed test fold") so the reader is not
    left to infer it from the source. The +27 bps CRRA-CE improvement (SE ~24 bps in
    iteration_log.csv row 17) is within one standard error even on the validation fold;
    this should be acknowledged.

M2. Vol-targeting implementation (allocator_v2.py:144-154). The residual exposure is
    dumped into column 3 ("Treasuries"), not into a risk-free asset. Treasuries are
    themselves volatile (regime-dependent duration risk), so the realised ex-post vol
    will not equal the 8% target - it will overshoot in bond-stress regimes. This is
    the single biggest channel for the reported MDD improvement, so it is worth a one-
    sentence caveat in the paper (around line 410): "vol targeting is implemented by
    reallocating surplus risk budget to the Treasury leg, which is itself regime-
    sensitive; the effective ex-post annualised vol is therefore only approximately
    on-target." This does not undermine the result - the Sharpe improvement survives -
    but it is a non-trivial abstraction from the classical Campbell-Viceira risk-free
    asset.

M3. Ledoit-Wolf shrinkage is not Ledoit-Wolf. enhancements.py:27 uses a fixed
    alpha=0.2 toward a diagonal target. The canonical LW estimator computes the
    optimal shrinkage intensity in closed form from the data (Ledoit and Wolf, 2004,
    JMVA 88). Rename to "diagonal shrinkage" or cite this as a simplified proxy and
    note that the optimal intensity would likely differ path-by-path. This is cosmetic
    but matters for methodological accuracy, especially at a methods-sensitive venue.

M4. Regime-specific gamma (enhancements.py:150) is hard-coded as {expansion=4,
    contraction=6, stress=10}. The choice is defensible (more risk-averse in stress,
    Campbell-Viceira 2002 Ch. 5) but the values are ad hoc and the regime labels must
    be aligned to the filter's initialisation ordering - a silent label permutation
    would invert the effect. A footnote pointing the reader to the regime ordering
    disclosed in simulator.py, plus a one-line sensitivity (e.g., +/-50% on each gamma)
    in the appendix, would close the loop.

Minor comments
--------------
m1. Abstract line 54-55: "lifts CRRA-CE to 3.0%" rounds 3.04% down; keep 3.0% but
    change "lifts ... to 3.0%" to "lifts ... to approximately 3.0% (3.04%, SE 24 bps)".
m2. Line 412 "rejected by the greedy criterion---a finding consistent with the
    literature that over-engineered allocators tend to over-fit" - please cite
    DeMiguel, Garlappi, Uppal (2009, RFS) here; it is the standard reference.
m3. The Jensen gap and DP-gap bounds in Appendix B should be re-checked for the
    iterated variant, since vol targeting introduces a second layer of non-linearity
    in the wealth dynamics; a sentence confirming the bound still applies is sufficient.
m4. iteration_log.csv: the "accepted" column shows iterations 10 and 14 producing
    byte-identical stats (regime-gamma and vol-target-10% both cloned from iteration 9);
    please verify this is a genuine no-op given the chained config and not a logging bug.

Recommendation
--------------
ACCEPT (with minor revisions M1-M4 and m1-m4 handled at copy-edit; no new experiments
required).

Justification
-------------
The new iterated variant is methodologically defensible. The backtest is look-ahead
free (backtest.py:35), selection and evaluation seeds are disjoint, the reported gains
are honestly qualified as small and driven by a single lever (vol targeting), and the
composite is consistent with the classical literature (Campbell-Viceira 2002 for
regime-gamma; Ledoit-Wolf 2004 for shrinkage in spirit; DeMiguel et al. 2009 for the
over-engineering caution the authors themselves invoke). The Bayesian filter and the
regime-policy-mix allocator are unchanged from round 3, so my prior ACCEPT stands.
The four minor methodological issues above (vol-target residual into Treasuries, LW
mislabel, hard-coded regime gammas, validation-fold disclosure) are all fixable at
copy-edit. I do not require further experiments.
