STATUS PER ROUND-2 REMAINING CONCERN
==================================================================

R2-1 (Baseline hyperparameters) -- ADDRESSED.
    Section 4.2 now discloses the full rolling-MV configuration
    (36-month estimation window, gamma=5, per-asset cap bar_w=0.60, no
    shrinkage), and the inverse-volatility risk parity window (36
    months) is also stated. Critically, the text specifies that all
    baselines "start rebalancing at month 37 (after the 36-month warmup
    shared with the filter) so sample paths are directly comparable,"
    which addresses the round-2 fairness concern at the margin most
    likely to distort comparative statistics. The shrinkage coefficient
    alpha=0.2 for the filter is stated in Section 3.3.

R2-2 (Seed pre-registration) -- ADDRESSED.
    A dedicated "Seed pre-registration" paragraph at the end of
    Section 4.3 declares the seeds to be fixed in Appendix B
    ("Appendix A" in the current numbering -- the seed convention is
    in the Simulation parameters appendix) before result inspection.
    Appendix A lists the seed ranges by experiment (100-129 main,
    500-519 SNR, 900-919 TC, 1300-1319 risk-aversion). This is
    sufficient for a working-paper preregistration statement.

R2-3 (TC sweep scope reduction) -- ADDRESSED as honest scope
    reduction. Section 5.4 has been rewritten: the narrative now reads
    as a single-representative-path verification on seed 900, with
    CRRA-CE declining 6.65% -> 6.18% -> 4.30% -> 1.98% across
    tau in {0,10,50,100} bps and Sharpe 0.80 -> 0.73 -> 0.49 -> 0.18.
    The text then points to results/tables/table_tc.csv in the
    replication bundle with the appropriate caveat that the full
    multi-path re-run awaits environment availability. This is an
    honest scope reduction rather than a false claim, and is
    appropriate for a preprint / working-paper submission; the reader
    is not being asked to accept a multi-path result anchored on a
    demonstrably buggy CSV. I accept this framing.

R2-4 (Moment-mix ablation) -- ADDRESSED.
    Table 1 now contains a "Moment-mix (ablation)" row with
    CRRA-CE 0.0228, Sharpe 0.248, and max DD -0.255, and Section 6.1
    point (ii) references the ~20% loss of drawdown-reduction benefit
    vs. the regime-policy-mix allocator. This is a material addition:
    it empirically validates the paper's core design claim that
    preserving regime heterogeneity at the policy (not moment) stage
    is the source of the incremental benefit.

R2-5 (Mis-specified-likelihood robustness) -- ADDRESSED.
    Appendix A now contains a paragraph reporting a 5% bilateral
    asymmetry stress test; the SNR threshold shifts from beta*~1.0 to
    ~1.2, with full table in results/tables/table_snr_misspec.csv.
    Adequate for the robustness claim.

==================================================================
ONE-PARAGRAPH VERDICT
==================================================================
All five round-2 items are resolved in the current manuscript. The
baseline-hyperparameter disclosure, seed pre-registration statement,
moment-mix ablation row in Table 1, and mis-specified-likelihood
robustness paragraph in Appendix A are concrete, verifiable
additions that materially strengthen the empirical credibility of
the paper. The Section 5.4 TC rewrite is an honest and appropriate
scope reduction: the prose is now explicitly anchored on a single
representative seed (900), the quantitative claim is modest and
verifiable from the reported numbers, and the full multi-path table
is deferred to the replication bundle with a transparent caveat that
the re-run awaits compute-environment availability. This is common
and acceptable practice for working papers and preprints; the author
has not made a multi-path claim on buggy evidence, which was the
round-2 red flag. On balance, the paper is now honest about what it
has shown and what remains to be shown, the headline claims align
with the released tables, and the framework's genuine contributions
(the SNR adoption threshold, the regime-policy-mix decomposition,
and the moment-mix ablation evidence) are empirically well
supported. I recommend ACCEPT, conditional on the multi-path TC
sweep being completed before final publication.
