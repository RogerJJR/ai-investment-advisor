NEW MINOR (WRITING / NUMERICAL-CONSISTENCY) COMMENTS
----------------------------------------------------
M1. TC paragraph, line 517: "the framework's lead over both benchmarks is
    preserved (79 bps over risk parity and 186 bps over 60/40 at tau=50 bps)".
    Recomputing from table_tc.csv at tc=50: NR=3.331%, RP=2.667%, 60/40=
    1.475%. NR - RP = 66.4 bps, NR - 60/40 = 185.6 bps. The 60/40 figure is
    fine (rounds to 186). The risk-parity lead should read "~66 bps" not
    "79 bps". This is the only stale number I could find.

M2. Table 1 (line 431 vs 434-435): EqualWeight ann. return 0.04592632 and
    SixtyForty 0.04593470 both print as "0.0459" at the displayed precision,
    but only 60/40 is bolded. The bold is technically correct (60/40 is
    marginally higher), yet a reader cannot distinguish the two values from
    the printed table. Consider either (a) printing four decimals
    (0.0459 vs 0.0459 -> 0.04593 vs 0.04593) or (b) adding a footnote
    "tied to displayed precision; 60/40 higher at full precision". Optional.

M3. SNR sensitivity, line 473: "at $\beta \ge 2$, the narrative filter
    strictly dominates". Table~\ref{tab:snr} shows at beta=4 Narrative
    CRRA-CE and RegimeOnly CRRA-CE both equal 0.0226 to four decimals,
    i.e. a tie rather than strict dominance at the printed precision. Soften
    to "at beta >= 2 the narrative filter dominates (strictly at beta=2,
    essentially tied at beta=4)". Optional.

M4. "Competitive ... not dominant" framing: I verified the introduction (line
    137-139: "risk parity remains a strong utility-based competitor on our
    baseline configuration") and conclusion (line 672-673: "competitive with
    inverse-volatility risk parity"). These are now consistent with the new
    story -- the *baseline* is competitive, the *iterated* variant dominates
    on CRRA-CE, Sharpe, and MDD per Table 1. No rewording needed, but the
    author may wish to add one sentence to the Conclusion acknowledging the
    iterated variant's dominance for completeness.

M5. Abstract/Table-1 consistency (verified): NR baseline Sharpe 0.30 (table
    0.302), NR baseline CRRA-CE 2.8% (table 2.77%), NR iterated Sharpe 0.32
    (table 0.320), CRRA-CE 3.0% (table 3.04%), MDD -18.7%, turnover 3.7% --
    all cross-reference cleanly. Iterated subsection line 407 "+27 bps to
    3.04%" is internally consistent with baseline 2.77%. The prose
    "reduces monthly turnover approximately in half (from 7.5% to 3.7%)" is
    accurate (7.53% -> 3.67%).

M6. No dangling references to "seed 900", "6.65%", or "30% monthly turnover"
    remain anywhere in the manuscript (grep-verified). The rolling MV entry
    in Table 1 correctly shows 25.8% turnover, not the former 30% figure.

M7. Figures fig_equity_paths.pdf, fig_regime_post.pdf, fig_snr_curve.pdf
    all present in results/figures/. All three \ref tags (fig:equity,
    fig:post, fig:snr) resolve. No orphaned subsections; the new
    "Iterated allocator variant" subsection is properly integrated between
    "Main comparison" and "Marginal contribution".

M8. References.bib: all \cite keys have matching entries (29 entries total).
    elsarticle-harv should compile cleanly; nothing new to flag.

M9. AI-usage declaration (Section 5.3 and ai_usage_declaration.md): unchanged
    from round 4, remains CRediT-aligned and explicit about the four internal
    peer-review rounds. Note that round 5 is now a *fifth* internal round;
    the declaration currently says "Four rounds" (line 652 in main.tex).
    Minor: either update to "five rounds" or generalise to "several rounds"
    before camera-ready. This is the one wording item that is directly
    load-bearing for honesty-of-disclosure.
