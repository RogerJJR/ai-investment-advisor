Reviewer #3 (AI/ML specialist, LLM applications in finance) -- ROUND 3
Manuscript: "Narrative Regimes: LLM-Augmented Strategic Asset Allocation for Long-Horizon Investors"
Journal: Decision Support Systems
Date: 2026-04-20

================================================================
STATUS PER ROUND-2 COMMENT
================================================================

R1 (Residual "eliminates look-ahead bias" phrase in Conclusion).
ADDRESSED. Section 7 (Conclusion, l.641-643) now reads "In a
Monte-Carlo study that is free of look-ahead contamination by
construction," which is the correct framing -- the simulator cannot
eliminate a bias that only attaches to empirical LLM evaluations, but
it can, by construction, avoid contamination from the LLM's own
training corpus. The Conclusion additionally flags (l.650-652) that
empirical replication should be "conducted with a trusted third party
to preclude implicit look-ahead during the development of the filter."
This is a strong, honest note that I did not explicitly request but
welcome; it pre-empts the obvious post-publication objection that the
author (who has seen the simulator's parameters) is not a neutral
party for empirical replication.

R2 (Beta-to-accuracy overlay on Figure 1). ADDRESSED. The Figure 1
caption (l.474-481) now includes the translation formula
beta = 1/sigma^2 where sigma is "the RMS residual of the LLM's
per-class scores from the true-class indicator," and states that this
lets a practitioner validate their institution's LLM before exposing
live capital. This is cleaner than the one-line footnote I suggested
and gives the reader a directly computable mapping from standard
classification diagnostics to the SNR grid. I consider this fully
resolved.

R3 (No confusion-matrix robustness experiment). UNCHANGED. The
authors continue to rely on disclosure in Section 5.2 ("Signal
abstraction") rather than run a confusion-matrix sweep. As noted in
round 2, I accept this; the disclosure is honest and the beta*
threshold is explicitly framed as a lower bound.

R4 (Taxonomy placement against Tang-Zhang 2024). ADDRESSED. The
Related Literature section (l.174-178) now states that in the
Tang-Zhang taxonomy, Narrative Regimes occupies "the unoccupied
intersection of macro-regime signal extraction, Bayesian belief
updating, and long-horizon utility-based allocation," and contrasts
this against the existing clusters (asset-level forecasting,
sentiment-to-signal pipelines, short-horizon RL). This is exactly the
cell-level placement I asked for and clinches the contribution's
novelty at a glance.

================================================================
REMAINING CONCERNS
================================================================

None that block publication. The confusion-matrix experiment (R3)
remains a suggested extension rather than a revision requirement.

================================================================
RECOMMENDATION
================================================================
ACCEPT

================================================================
JUSTIFICATION
================================================================
All four round-2 textual items are resolved and the one substantive
non-textual item (R3) was explicitly accepted as a disclosure-only
route in round 2. The paper's framing is now fully internally
consistent: internal-validity claims are clearly separated from
external-validity claims; the beta threshold is anchored to a
measurable LLM-pipeline diagnostic; the Tang-Zhang cell placement
resolves the novelty question. The AI-usage declaration is
Elsevier-compliant. I recommend acceptance.
