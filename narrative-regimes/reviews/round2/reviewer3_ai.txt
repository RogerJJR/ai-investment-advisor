Reviewer #3 (AI/ML specialist, LLM applications in finance) -- ROUND 2
Manuscript: "Narrative Regimes: LLM-Augmented Strategic Asset Allocation for Long-Horizon Investors"
Journal: Decision Support Systems
Date: 2026-04-20

================================================================
STATUS PER ROUND-1 CONCERN
================================================================

M1 (Signal abstraction mismatch -- confusion matrices, AR(1) noise,
calibration drift). PARTIALLY ADDRESSED. Section 5.2 ("Signal
abstraction") now explicitly enumerates the three failure modes I
flagged (non-isotropic confusion, temporal autocorrelation, calibration
drift near transitions) and reframes beta* ~ 1 as a lower bound on the
practical adoption threshold. This is the honest move. However, the
authors opted for option (b) -- disclosure -- rather than option (a) --
a confusion-matrix/AR(1) robustness experiment. Given that such an
experiment is a one-day addition to simulator.py and would materially
strengthen the external-validity argument, I regard this as acceptable
for a first publication but noted as a remaining soft weakness.

M2 (SNR calibration to 2025 LLM quality). NOT DIRECTLY ADDRESSED. The
beta grid {0.25, ..., 4} is unchanged, and I see no overlay of published
LLM macro-classification accuracy against the sweep in Figure 1 or
Table 3. That said, the authors' addition of Kim-Muhn-Nikolaev 2024 and
Fatouros 2024 citations implicitly anchors the reader to the relevant
empirical literature, and the abstract/results language no longer claims
"strict dominance" -- positioning Narrative Regimes as "competitive, not
dominant" relative to risk parity. This defuses the most concerning
overclaim even without a beta-to-accuracy mapping. I still recommend
adding a single sentence near Figure 1 stating that beta in [0.5, 1.5]
corresponds to the F1 range of current production LLM pipelines.

M3 (Look-ahead bias overclaim). ADDRESSED. The Contributions list
(item 2) now reads "establishes *internal* consistency -- it is
complementary to, not a substitute for, empirical replication," and
Section 5.2 opens with "Simulation internal validity, not external."
This is the correct framing. One residue: the Conclusion still contains
"eliminates look-ahead bias by construction," which should be softened
to "eliminates LLM corpus contamination by construction" for consistency.

M4 (Differentiation from Chen et al. 2024 LLM-Black-Litterman).
MINIMALLY ADDRESSED. The related-work paragraph now states the
integration-level distinction ("embed the LLM signal within a Bayesian
regime-filter rather than as a direct asset-level view"), which is the
intellectual core I requested. A side-by-side inference diagram was not
added, but the verbal differentiation is now sufficient for a reader to
locate the contribution in the signal-integration spectrum.

M5 (AI usage declaration -- Elsevier compliance). ADDRESSED. Section 6.3
now names "Claude Opus 4.6 by Anthropic, accessed via the Claude desktop
application in April 2026," provides CRediT-aligned role disclosure
(Conceptualization, Software, Writing, Visualization), states that the
author independently verified results, confirms the LLM is not listed as
an author, and discloses that two non-trivial bugs were caught during
human verification. This exceeds Elsevier's minimum bar.

M6 (Tang & Zhang 2024 survey engagement). NOT ADDRESSED. The citation
remains a single sentence and the paper still does not state which
taxonomy cell Narrative Regimes occupies. Minor.

================================================================
REMAINING ISSUES
================================================================

R1. One residual "eliminates look-ahead bias by construction" phrase in
Section 7 (Conclusion) contradicts the corrected framing elsewhere.
Trivial fix.

R2. No beta-to-accuracy overlay on Figure 1. Can be a one-line footnote.

R3. No confusion-matrix robustness experiment. I accept the disclosure-
only route but note it as a limitation for readers.

R4. Taxonomy placement against Tang & Zhang (2024) still absent.

================================================================
RECOMMENDATION
================================================================
MINOR REVISION

================================================================
JUSTIFICATION
================================================================
The authors have honestly engaged with the three most substantive
round-1 concerns: the signal abstraction is now properly flagged, the
look-ahead claim is properly circumscribed as internal consistency, and
the repositioning of the framework as "competitive, not dominant"
against risk parity removes the overclaim that motivated my M2. The AI
usage declaration is now Elsevier-compliant with model version, access
window, and CRediT role disclosure. The remaining issues are textual
(one residual phrase, one figure footnote, one taxonomy sentence) and
can be handled in a minor-revision round without further refereeing.
The absence of a confusion-matrix robustness experiment is the one
place where I would, in a perfect world, have preferred an additional
simulation, but the transparent disclosure in Section 5.2 combined with
the deflated headline claims makes the paper defensible as it stands.
I recommend MINOR REVISION.
