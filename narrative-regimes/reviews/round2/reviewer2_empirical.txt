Reviewer #2 (Empirical Finance / Financial Econometrics) — ROUND 2
Manuscript: "Narrative Regimes: LLM-Augmented Strategic Asset Allocation for Long-Horizon Investors"
Venue: Decision Support Systems

==================================================================
STATUS PER MAJOR ROUND-1 COMMENT
==================================================================

M1 (headline Sharpe mismatch) — ADDRESSED.
    The abstract now reports Sharpe 0.26 (SE 0.03), which matches
    table_main.csv (sharpe_mean = 0.2567, n=30). Risk parity (0.28) is
    correctly flagged as comparable/superior on Sharpe, and the CRRA-CE
    comparison explicitly states risk parity beats Narrative Regimes by
    0.2 pp (2.6% vs. 2.4%). The repositioning as "competitive, not
    dominant; decision-transparent alternative to risk parity" is an
    honest and defensible framing.

M2 (transaction-cost sweep) — PARTIAL / NOT.
    This is the remaining concern. The released table_tc.csv is STILL
    the stale version: annual_return, annual_vol, sharpe, sortino,
    avg_turnover, and crra_ce_annual remain byte-identical across
    tc_bps ∈ {0,5,10,20,50}; only cagr, max_drawdown, and calmar vary.
    The paper replaces the multi-path CRRA narrative with a single-
    seed (seed 900) verification showing CRRA-CE 6.65% → 1.98% as tau
    rises 0 → 100 bps, and defers the multi-path table to the
    replication bundle. The author has at least made the disclosure
    transparent (Section 5.4 explicitly says the multi-path table is in
    the bundle), but this leaves the reader unable to verify the
    central-tendency claim from released data. A single seed is an
    anecdote, not a robustness check. The fact that the stale file is
    still shipped in results/tables/ — and contains the same bug I
    flagged in round 1 — is a reproducibility red flag, even with the
    caveat.

M3 (paired MC standard errors) — ADDRESSED.
    Table 1 now reports (mean, SE) for every metric; the Sharpe SE of
    0.028 and the CRRA-CE SE of 0.002 make clear that Narrative
    Regimes and Risk Parity are statistically indistinguishable at 5%
    on Sharpe, and that the CRRA-CE gap is within 1 SE. The text
    correctly describes this. The accuracy gap (+11.0 pp, SE 2.8 pp)
    is likewise credibly bounded.

M4 (baseline fairness / hyperparameters) — PARTIAL.
    Rolling-MV window length and shrinkage parameters are still not
    disclosed in the text; alpha=0.2 is now stated but the weight
    inertia lambda=0.7 is only given for Narrative Regimes (not
    clarified whether it applies to rolling MV). No HRP or min-variance
    baseline added. Given the repositioning to "competitive not
    dominant," this gap is less load-bearing than in round 1.

M5 (DGP external validity) — NOT addressed, but Limitations section
    now explicitly acknowledges this and frames the contribution as
    internal consistency + decision rule, which is defensible for a DSS
    framework paper.

M6 (look-ahead-freedom semantic over-reach) — ADDRESSED.
    Limitations paragraph now states the simulation-definitional nature
    of the claim in plain language.

==================================================================
REMAINING CHERRY-PICKING CONCERNS
==================================================================

(i) The TC narrative is now anchored on seed 900, chosen — by the
    author's own seed convention (Appendix A) — as the first seed of
    the TC sweep. Without the multi-path table, a reader cannot rule
    out that seed 900 happens to be a favourable draw. Given that the
    per-seed CRRA-CE at tau=0 in the stale CSV ranges from 0.017 to
    0.067 across the ten NR rows, seed 900 sits near the top of that
    distribution.
(ii) Seed-42 posterior figure remains a single-seed illustration.
    Minor but worth an appendix distribution plot.
(iii) No preregistration statement re: seed selection (round-1 m2).

RECOMMENDATION
==================================================================
MINOR REVISION (conditional)

==================================================================
JUSTIFICATION
==================================================================
The round-1 headline-integrity problems (M1, M3, M6) are fully
resolved. The abstract and text now agree with table_main.csv, paired
MC standard errors are reported, and the look-ahead framing is honest.
The repositioning to a decision-transparent, competitive-not-dominant
framework is an appropriate response that preserves the paper's
genuine contribution (the SNR adoption threshold and the filter +
regime-policy-mix decomposition).

The remaining concern is the TC sweep (M2). The stale multi-path
table in results/tables/table_tc.csv has a visible specification bug
(Sharpe/CRRA-CE invariant to tc_bps) that will be spotted by any
reviewer who opens the CSV; shipping it unchanged while the prose
discusses a single seed is a credibility risk even with honest
disclosure. For acceptance I require: (a) re-running the TC sweep with
costs deducted at the per-period return level and replacing the CSV,
or (b) removing table_tc.csv from the released artefacts and
relabelling Section 5.4 as a single-path sensitivity illustration,
deferring the multi-path claim to a clearly-labelled future-work note.
Either is acceptable; (a) is strongly preferred. Disclosure of
rolling-MV hyperparameters and a one-line preregistration note on
seed selection should also be added.

Given that all other revisions are substantive and honest, and the
remaining fix is a bounded pipeline correction or a textual hedge,
minor revision is warranted. Reject-level concerns from round 1 have
been retired.
