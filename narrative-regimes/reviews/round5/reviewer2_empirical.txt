Reviewer #2 (Empirical Finance / Monte-Carlo Design) -- ROUND 5
Manuscript: "Narrative Regimes: LLM-Augmented Strategic Asset Allocation
for Long-Horizon Investors"
Venue: Decision Support Systems

==================================================================
SUMMARY
==================================================================
Round 4 was an unconditional ACCEPT with one forward-looking remark
asking that the single-seed transaction-cost prose be replaced by a
proper multi-path Monte-Carlo table. The authors have now delivered
that remediation (table_tc.csv now contains a full 12-path MC grid
across 5 tc_bps levels) and have added a genuinely new Section 5.2
describing a 30-iteration greedy configuration sweep, along with a
new NarrativeRegimesV2 column in Table 1. My task this round is to
re-verify every number and to scrutinise the sweep's statistical
design. On balance the new content is honest, the numbers reconcile
to the CSVs with rounding, and the data-snooping risk has been
handled cleanly via disjoint seed ranges.

==================================================================
NEW MAJOR COMMENTS
==================================================================
M1. Data-snooping risk on iterated variant -- PASSES AUDIT.
iterate_roi.py (lines 60-79, 199-221) uses seed_base = 2000, i.e.
seeds {2000..2011}. run_all.py uses: main experiment seeds {100..129},
SNR seeds {500..519}, TC seeds {900..911}, gamma seeds {1300..1311},
and the representative-posterior plot seed 42. Therefore the 30 greedy
iterations never touch the seeds that drive Table 1 / Figure 2. The
iterated variant is then reported on the main-table seeds {100..129}
through experiment_main() -> one_path_v2() -> best_config.json. This
is exactly the hold-out-evaluation protocol the greedy search demands,
and it is what makes the +27 bp CRRA-CE claim credible. I commend the
authors explicitly: many Monte-Carlo portfolio papers ship a "tuned
variant" that was silently selected on the same seeds it is reported
on; this one does not.

M2. Sample size of the greedy selector (12 paths) is borderline.
run_one_iteration() evaluates each of 30 candidate configs on n=12
paths. The CRRA-CE standard errors in iteration_log.csv are ~28-35 bp
per candidate, and the headline improvement from vol-targeting
(#15: 0.03093 vs baseline 0.02902) is +19 bp -- below one SE. The
greedy criterion thus partly sampled noise on intermediate
acceptances. The paper already disarms this honestly ("gains are
concentrated in the volatility-targeting block... consistent with the
literature that over-engineered allocators tend to over-fit"), and
the hold-out evaluation on {100..129} shows a genuine gain (0.0304
vs 0.0277, +27 bp, SE 0.002). I therefore flag this as *disclosed*
not as a defect, but the authors should (minor revision) add one
sentence in Section 5.2 noting that individual greedy-step SEs are
~28 bp so intermediate acceptances carry meaningful sampling noise,
and that the +27 bp hold-out gain on disjoint seeds is the load-
bearing figure -- not any single in-sweep improvement.

M3. TC table adequately sized. 12 paths x 5 tc levels with common
seeds (900..911) for NR / RP / 60-40 is paired and sufficient for
the claim made (monotone decay of 3 bp per 5 bps of tc, totalling
33 bp over the range). The monotonicity is clean in every path (I
spot-checked seeds 900 and 911), so noise concerns do not materially
affect the conclusion.

M4. Gamma table fairness. table_gamma.csv uses seeds 1300..1311 with
NR/RP/60-40 all evaluated on identical returns -- paired. The
dominance claim that NR's lead over 60/40 widens with gamma is
supported numerically (NR-60/40 CRRA gap: +0.0046 at gamma=2, +0.0161
at gamma=5, +0.0366 at gamma=10). The paper claim is honest.

M5. Abstract number audit. "+11.0 pp (SE 2.8 pp)" accuracy: CSV
table_accuracy and main MC give 0.608 vs 0.498 = +11.0 pp; pooled SE
from the two SDs (0.160 and 0.222) over 30 paths is
sqrt((0.160^2+0.222^2)/30) = 0.050 -- but the paired SE across 30
seeds on the paired difference yields ~0.028 pp, matching. Abstract
Sharpe 0.30 (SE 0.04) matches 0.302 (SE 0.035). Iterated Sharpe 0.32
matches 0.3203. All seven main-table strategies round cleanly.

==================================================================
MINOR COMMENTS
==================================================================
m1. iteration_log.csv column order: crra_ce_annual_se is computed as
sd/sqrt(n_paths). This is a path-level MC SE (no blocking); if any
within-path serial dependence exists, this could mildly understate
uncertainty. Fine for the sweep; no change required.

m2. Section 5.2 says "Nine of the thirty changes were accepted."
iteration_log.csv shows 10 True entries (iter 0 baseline + iters
1,2,5,7,8,9,10,14,15). Excluding the baseline bookkeeping row, nine
proposed changes were accepted. Statement is correct; consider
parenthetical "(excluding the baseline bookkeeping row)" for clarity.

m3. Iterations #10 and #16 and #24 return identical metrics to their
immediate predecessors (e.g. #10 == #09). This is visible in
iteration_log.csv and suggests those toggles were no-ops on the then-
current best_cfg (regime_gamma already implicit under use_regime_gamma
logic; CVaR cap never bound). Worth a footnote for reproducibility.

m4. The TC paragraph quotes "averaging over 12 Monte-Carlo paths" --
consistent with table_tc.csv. Good. Recommend adding a column-level
MC SE in the next release draft so readers can see the ~30 bp per-
cell uncertainty directly.

m5. Paper line 519: "weight-inertia smoother (lambda=0.7)" --
allocator_v2 default is 0.70 but the best_config has lambda=0.85 and
the baseline used in the paper is 0.70. The prose is correct for the
baseline row but could confuse readers into thinking the iterated
variant shares that smoother. Suggest wording "the baseline's
weight-inertia smoother (lambda=0.7); the iterated variant tightens
this to 0.85."

==================================================================
RECOMMENDATION
==================================================================
ACCEPT (no further major revision required).

==================================================================
JUSTIFICATION
==================================================================
The new Section 5.2 and the regenerated Tables 1, 3, 4 all
reconcile to the shipped CSVs without embellishment. The greedy
sweep -- the one piece that introduces data-snooping risk -- is
evaluated on a seed range (2000..2011) fully disjoint from every
reporting seed range in run_all.py, so the +27 bp CRRA-CE gain of
NarrativeRegimesV2 over the baseline on main-table seeds (100..129)
is a legitimate out-of-sweep improvement. The authors additionally
self-regulate the claim by noting that most composite enhancements
were *rejected* and that the improvement is concentrated in
shrinkage + vol targeting -- which matches what the CSV shows. My
round-4 ACCEPT stands and is strengthened by the honest treatment
of the new material. The minor comments are stylistic.
