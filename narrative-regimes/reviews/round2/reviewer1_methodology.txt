Reviewer #1 Report (Round 2) - Methodology / Bayesian Filtering / Portfolio Optimization
Manuscript: "Narrative Regimes: LLM-Augmented Strategic Asset Allocation for Long-Horizon Investors"
Venue: Decision Support Systems

I thank the author for a careful, responsive revision. I evaluate each of my round-1 major comments below.

RESOLUTION OF ROUND-1 MAJOR COMMENTS

1. Overstated CRRA-optimality of the regime-policy-mix allocator.
   ADDRESSED. Section 3.3 now correctly acknowledges that Jensen's inequality precludes a first-order approximation claim, reframes the allocator as a "heuristic decomposition" (preserving regime heterogeneity, exact at point-mass posteriors, interpretable as Bayesian model averaging), and Appendix A (app:jensen) quantifies the CRRA-CE gap against an exact finite-horizon DP solution on a reduced three-asset problem (mean ~14 bps/yr, IQR 5-22 bps; grows to ~50 bps under diffuse posteriors, <5 bps under concentrated ones). This is exactly the quantification I requested and, helpfully, the appendix also tells practitioners when the gap matters. Satisfactory.

2. Narrative likelihood shares parametric form with the DGP.
   PARTIALLY ADDRESSED. The Limitations subsection now discusses the self-fulfilling concern explicitly and reports a mis-specified-likelihood robustness check (5% bilateral asymmetry in the filter's signal prior) that leaves the conclusions unchanged. This is a good-faith response. However, (i) only results are mentioned; the experiment parameters and tables live in the replication bundle rather than the main text or appendix, and (ii) a 5% mean-asymmetry is a mild perturbation relative to the richer mis-specifications I suggested (heteroskedastic noise, correlated signal errors, temporally autocorrelated errors, confusion-matrix-style non-isotropy). I would accept the current level as sufficient for publication given the simulation-internal framing, but a half-page summary table in the appendix would strengthen the claim materially.

3. Ad-hoc EMA transition-matrix update vs. Dirichlet soft-count.
   NOT ADDRESSED. The code still contains the EMA-of-rank-1-outer-product update (regime_filter.py lines 142-145) and the paper only alludes to it as "smoothed co-occurrence estimator." There is no justification as a stochastic-approximation EM step and no sensitivity comparison against a Dirichlet soft-count alternative. This remains a soft concern: P only appears multiplicatively in the Markov predict step, and the reported results do not hinge on it sharply, so it does not block acceptance, but a one-paragraph justification (or a 2-line sensitivity appendix) is still warranted.

4. Welford recursion ESS safeguard.
   NOT ADDRESSED. allocator / filter code still accumulates sum_w, sum_wx, sum_wxx without an ESS floor or informative inverse-Wishart prior; only the 1e-6 I ridge is applied. Per-regime ESS for the stress regime can remain small for long windows, and CRRA-CE under gamma=5 is materially sensitive to the stress-regime covariance tail. A lightweight fix (report minimum per-regime ESS; impose an IW prior of strength e.g. 20 months) would be a further improvement. I will not make this blocking, but flag it for the record.

5. CRRA-CE annualization.
   NOT ADDRESSED. backtest.py lines 91-97 still computes monthly CE and then annualizes via (1+ce_monthly)^12 - 1, which is exact only for log utility (gamma=1). The paper does not state this approximation explicitly or report the exact time-aggregated CE. For gamma=5 over 360 months the bias is small but non-zero and is directly relevant to the headline "2.4% vs 2.6% for risk parity" comparison. A one-sentence note in Section 4.3 plus the exact figure in the bundle would fully resolve this. This is the single largest remaining minor-major item.

6. Look-ahead audit & common random numbers.
   ADDRESSED. Paired Monte-Carlo standard errors are now reported (Table 1), which implicitly confirms CRN across strategies. The information-partition logic in the filter vs. the shift(1) in backtest.py remains correct.

REMAINING MINOR ISSUES
- "Smoothed co-occurrence estimator" in Section 3.2 should name the algorithm and cite (stochastic-approximation EM or online Baum-Welch).
- allocator.py line 125 still has `from copy import copy` inside the loop; cosmetic.
- Standard errors on the ablation (Table 2) use per-path SD rather than paired-MC SE; paired SE would be tighter and more informative.
- Appendix A should state which DP discretization and boundary conditions are used (25x25x25 grid is quoted but not the interpolation scheme).

RECOMMENDATION: MINOR REVISION

JUSTIFICATION
The revision substantially strengthens the paper. The central theoretical issue (comment 1) is fully resolved in a disciplined way that also quantifies the heuristic's cost. The self-fulfilling concern (comment 2) is acknowledged in-text and addressed by a robustness check, albeit a modest one. Paired-MC SEs now support the statistical claims honestly. Three previously-raised items (EMA transition update, Welford ESS, CRRA-CE annualization) remain technically unfixed but are small-impact relative to the headline results and can be handled by brief textual clarifications plus supplementary runs in the bundle. Subject to: (i) explicit disclosure of the gamma-not-equal-to-1 CE-annualization approximation and the exact time-aggregated figure; (ii) a Dirichlet soft-count sensitivity row for P; (iii) one row for moment-mix in Table 1; and (iv) either an in-appendix table for the mis-specified-likelihood check or a pointer with result summary, I would support acceptance.
